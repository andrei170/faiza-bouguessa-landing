The Atelier

Before the brand, a grandmother and a sewing machine.

Faiza Bouguessa learned to notice fabric early, watching a grandmother who sewed. The path was not a straight line. There were years in the air as cabin crew, a quiet pull toward design that kept knocking, and finally, in 2014, an atelier in Dubai.

The idea was simple and a little contrary. Design for the woman no one was thinking about. Make pieces that feel like something when you wear them. Choose restraint over noise.

"I like to think of the brand as a kind of quiet power. Something strong, but not loud."

That instinct turned into a label worn on the world's stages and carried in the world's wardrobes. The work speaks softly. It has never needed to shout.
